# The world is much taller — what that means for you

Archean Rise builds a world from **Y −256 to Y 768**. Vanilla is −64 to 320. Mountains reach 708.

## You must pick the world type when the world is created

The height is locked in the moment a world is made, and **it can never be changed afterwards**. That's a
Minecraft limitation, not ours.

- **Singleplayer:** Create New World → *World* tab → *World Type* → **Archean Rise**
- **Server:** put `level-type=archean_rise:archean_rise` in `server.properties` **before the first start**

Skip this and the mod does nothing — you just get a normal world. Adding the mod to a world that already
exists changes nothing either. You need a **new** world.

## Three things worth knowing before you dig

**Caves stay where vanilla puts them.** All that extra depth is mostly solid rock — below roughly Y −48
there is very little cave. Proper deep caves are planned, but they aren't here yet.

**The deepest layer is the best layer — on purpose.** Diamond is most common right down at **Y −256**.
That's a long trip through solid rock, and it's meant to be.

**Bigger chunks cost more to generate.** Each chunk is about 2.7× the volume of a vanilla one, so
exploring new land is heavier on your CPU. **C2ME** helps a lot. **Distant Horizons** is well worth it —
the world is big, and you'll want to see it.

## Old worlds

Worlds made before version 0.3.0 (the ones with "tiers") **will not load**. If you have one, keep it on
mod version 0.2.26.
